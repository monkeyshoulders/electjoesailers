I'm Joe Sailers I want to represent you as a Huntersville Town Commissioner

As a resident of Huntersville for over 45 years, I've watched this town grow from a small country suburb, to a thriving regional destination for businesses and growing families. Huntersville faces some real challenges now and in the near future. Our infrastructure hasn't kept pace with our rapid growth. I know Huntersville can be the best town in North Carolina and we need the best leadership to get us there. Please stand with and Support me in making our town everything it can be.

The Issues

Economic Growth

A growing economy helps keep tax rates lower, but that growth must be good for Huntersville, not just for sake of growth. Nobody wants to make that I-77 daily commute and that's why I want to invite new business to expand here. I want to increase the Huntersville job market so you can work, live, and play here.

Schools

I am a product of CMS, graduating from North Meck High in 1967. I also attended Alexander Jr. High and Statesville Rd. Elementary. Education is vital to a strong and successful community. I will be a voice for Huntersville to the CMS Board to ensure that our community and children are set up for success.

Budgets

20+ years as a Cost Accountant for the City of Charlotte and managing a multi-million dollar construction fund, I learned what it means to be fiscally responsible. When dealing with such a fund keeping track of spending is paramount. I'm a fiscal conservative, and I believe that we must be true to Huntersville and it's growth.

Infrastructure... Roads roads roads.

It's no secret that we are behind on supporting our existing transportation system. We must make it a priority to catch up to where we need to be and make responsible decisions for future projects. We all feel the effects of lacking infrastructure from commute times to car maintenance. I will work with the state and the county as best as I can to improve our town.
